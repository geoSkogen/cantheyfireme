At-will(pg. 16)

We are an "at-will" employer. That means you are free to quit at any time, for any reason, just as New Seasons Market is free to terminate your employment at any time, for any reason not prohibited by law. No staff member or company representative, other than the president/CEO or a person designated by him or her in a signed employment agreement, has the authority to change the at-will employment relationship or to contract with any staff member for different terms of employment.

      Anti-Solicitation Policy

      Our goal is to have a positive and productive workplace where staff and customers alike feel comfortable. Approaching fellow staff in the workplace regarding activities, organizations, or causes, regardless of how worthwhile, important, or benevolent, can sometimes create unnecessary apprehension and pressures for fellow staff. In order to prevent disruption of our daily operations and to help ensure we maintain an upbeat and productive working environment, we have established the following rules around the solicitation of individuals and the distribution of materials on company property.

      What does "solicitation" mean?

      For purposes of this policy, “solicitation” means activity that prompts an immediate response from the staff being solicited; it is more than having a conversation with a co-worker, rather it is activity that is intended to get someone to do something (for example, sign a petition, buy a ticket to an event, or donate to a cause).
      +
        
      Non-solicitation do’s and don’ts:
      • Staff may solicit or promote support for any cause or organization so long as that solicitation does not take place during the staff member’s working time, or during the working time of the staff member at whom the activity is directed.
      • Staff may distribute or circulate any written or printed material in non-work areas during the staff member’s non-working time, (and in work areas so long as it is not during the working time of the staff member distributing material, or during the working time of the staff member or staff member at whom such activity is directed.)
      • Individuals who are not employed by New Seasons Market may not solicit on company property at any time for any purpose and . . .
      • Individuals who are not employed by New Seasons Market may only distribute literature on company property at the request of NSM and for such purposes as the core business of the company, such as the promotion or sampling of products we sell, or for approved events that are organized and managed by NSM, such as Earth Day events where an environmental group may distribute recycling, composting or gardening literature.

      How is working time defined?
      “Working time” includes all time for which a staff member is being paid to perform services for the company, but does not include breaks or meal periods. Because we care about keeping all areas of the store clean and orderly, the distribution of literature in non-working areas must occur in a way that does not result in excess litter.

      Staff bulletin boards
      • Information of special interest to all staff is posted regularly on company bulletin boards. These boards are located in areas convenient to all staff members, for example, in break rooms and near the time clock.
      • Some bulletin boards are for general posting. Staff may post information on these bulletin boards, however the materials are subject to the company’s anti-discrimination and anti-harassment policies.
      • Some bulletin boards are for specific purposes and committees, such as new policies, events, schedule posting, and others are committee boards such as green team, work safety and food safety. Staff may post information on these bulletin boards that is pertinent to the boards’ function; however the materials are subject to the company’s anti-discrimination and anti-harassment policies. Misleading information or information not pertinent to the bulletins board function will be removed[sic].

Staff may directly give their address and phone number to others but NSM may not.

      Social Media Policy

      This policy applies to all staff for job-related social media use, for personal, off-work hours, and non-work-related use. We encourage staff to speak up and join the conversation while following this policy. We have a lot of creative staff at New Seasons Market who may choose to express themselves through their own personal social media posts. We respect your right to choose to do this on your own time. Sometimes problems can arise when a personal post appears to be associated with New Seasons Market, or when a personal post is used in ways that violate the rights of others.

      Our social media outlets are an extension of the sales floor, so we ask that you not say anything online that you wouldn’t say to our customers in person. Social media outlets are great places for positive, helpful interactions. If you have grievances about your job, your coworkers, or company policy, it’s appropriate to address them directly with your store manager or a member of HR. Be honest, and always consider the possible outcomes of the comments that you make.

      In order to ensure that we avoid any misunderstandings or other problems, the following rules apply to social media use. Violation of these rules may result in corrective action and/or termination of employment, as well as legal action, when appropriate:
      • You may not use New Seasons Market property to create or maintain social media sites or postings, and you may not do so on work time or with New Seasons Market systems or equipment.
      • If you identify yourself as a staff member of New Seasons Market, whether by explicit statement or by implication, you will need to include that the views expressed are yours, and do not necessarily reflect the views of New Seasons Market.
      • All New Seasons Market policies and our code of conduct apply to anything that you put on social media.
      • You may not use for commercial purposes any NSM logos, intellectual property, marketing materials , images, or other promotional material.
      • You may not disclose any confidential information as defined elsewhere in this handbook
      • You must follow copyright and other intellectual property and trade secrets laws and not disclose or misuse such information
      • You must not engage in any online sabotage, bullying, slander, discrimination or harassment or use social media in violation of our other policies in this handbook
      • You may not use social media in ways that are malicious, obscene, threatening or intimidating, way[sic] that are meant to harm someone’s reputation, or for similar inappropriate or unlawful conduct
